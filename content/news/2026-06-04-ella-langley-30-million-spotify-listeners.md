We've been keeping an eye on Ella's streaming numbers all year, and they just hit a mark that's hard to wrap your head around. As of this morning, she's past 30 million monthly listeners on Spotify. On the country side of the platform, the only name still ahead of her is Morgan Wallen.

Parade reported the milestone Thursday, and it lands the way a lot of Ella's 2026 has — quietly enormous. Thirty million is the kind of figure that, a couple of years ago, you'd have filed next to the genre's permanent fixtures: Wallen, Zach Bryan, Luke Combs, Chris Stapleton. She's sitting in that company now, and she got there fast. Whiskey Riff flagged back in late April that she'd already passed everyone in country except Wallen on the platform. The needle hasn't stopped moving since.

What makes this one click is that it isn't a chart that resets every Tuesday. Monthly listeners are a measure of how many actual people are pulling up her catalog week after week — the radio crossover crowd, the "Choosin' Texas" obsessives, the folks who found Dandelion in April and never left. The album debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200, and the songs around it have done the slow, real work of turning a breakout into a habit.

It also tracks with everything else happening this summer. She's deep into her own Dandelion headline run while stacking stadium nights opening for Wallen, so her music is hitting two completely different audiences at once — her rooms and his. That kind of exposure is exactly how a number like this keeps climbing. If you've been meaning to catch her live, the full routing is on our [tour page](/tour), and we broke down [how she's juggling both tours at once](/news/2026-06-02-summer-two-tours-ahead) earlier this week.

We said on [June 1 that she had the inside track on song of the summer](/news/2026-06-01-song-of-the-summer-watch), and milestones like this are why. Streaming is the engine underneath all of it, and right now Ella's is running about as hot as anyone's in the genre. Second only to Wallen, climbing in the middle of a tour — that's a pretty good place to be heading into June.
