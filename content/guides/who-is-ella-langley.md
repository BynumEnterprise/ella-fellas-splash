## The two-sentence version

Ella Langley is a country singer-songwriter from Hope Hull, Alabama, who broke through in 2024 with the duet "you look like you love me" (with Riley Green) and has become one of the biggest artists in the format. By 2026 she had released her second album, Dandelion, and stepped onto stadium stages -- including as direct support on Morgan Wallen's Still The Problem Tour.

That is the elevator pitch. Now here is how to actually get into her before the show.

## Her biggest songs

If you want to walk into the stadium already knowing the words, start here:

- **"you look like you love me" (with Riley Green)** -- the duet that did it. It went Platinum and turned her into a household name. The chemistry between the two voices is the whole thing. This is song one.
- **"Choosin' Texas"** -- her biggest commercial moment, co-written with Miranda Lambert. Three minutes flat, hook in the first 45 seconds, built to sing along to in a car full of friends.
- **"weren't for the wind"** -- a signature single and one of the songs that made country radio pay attention.
- **"be her"** -- a Lori McKenna co-write off Dandelion and the album's emotional center. The song that proves she is a writer, not just a voice.
- **"don't mind if i do" and "paint the town blue"** -- two more Riley Green collaborations, if the first duet hooked you.

## Why she is worth showing up early for

Here is the thing about "direct support." On a stadium bill it is the slot right before the headliner -- the act that goes on last among the openers, after dark, to a stadium that is already full. That is Ella's slot on these Wallen dates. It is not a soundcheck nobody watches. It is a real set, with a real light show, in front of a real crowd.
