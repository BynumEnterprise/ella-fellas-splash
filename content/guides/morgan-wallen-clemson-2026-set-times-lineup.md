So when Morgan Wallen brings the Still The Problem Tour to Clemson's Memorial Stadium on June 27, 2026, it is a genuine event -- one of the biggest nights of the summer in upstate South Carolina. And the act in the prime slot right before Wallen is Ella Langley. If you have been waiting to see her on a stage this size, this is the one.

## The lineup, top to bottom

Here is the running order for the June 27 Memorial Stadium date:

- **Morgan Wallen** -- headliner
- **Ella Langley** -- direct support (on right before Wallen)
- **Gavin Adcock**
- **Jason Scott and the High Heat**

Direct support is the spot you want to catch. Ella goes on last among the openers, after dark, to a stadium that is already packed -- a full set with the production behind it, not a half-empty warmup. Gavin Adcock brings the rowdy energy earlier in the evening, and Jason Scott and the High Heat open things up.

## What we actually know about set times

We will not fabricate exact set times -- for stadium shows they usually are not published until the date is close. Here is what is real:

- The show is listed with doors around 4:00 PM and a show start around 5:30 PM.
- Once the show starts, openers run back to back, each playing a condensed set.

Bottom line: to see Ella Langley, be inside and in your spot by the listed show start, not Wallen's stage time. The openers move quickly.

## The venue: Memorial Stadium (Death Valley)

Memorial Stadium sits on the Clemson University campus and seats more than 80,000 -- this is a football stadium, not an arena, so plan accordingly. It is open-air, and late June in South Carolina means heat and humidity early, cooling once the sun drops. Hydrate, dress light, and bring a layer for after dark.
