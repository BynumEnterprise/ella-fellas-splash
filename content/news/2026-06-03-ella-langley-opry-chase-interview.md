Most of what we write about Ella lately is chart numbers and tour routing, so a quiet one is a nice change of pace. The Hollywood Reporter ran an interview with her on June 2, pegged to a private show she played at the Grand Ole Opry — a Chase Sapphire Reserve cardmember event with about 100 guests seated right on the Opry stage for a showcase and dinner. It's the kind of small room she doesn't get to play much anymore, and the conversation around it is the most relaxed we've heard her sound in a while.

## The celebrity-fan question got a good answer

Asked about famous people reaching out, Ella told THR that John Mayer getting in touch was the one that genuinely surprised her. That's a fun thing to sit with — an artist who two years ago was playing the daytime stages around Nashville now fielding notes from the guy who wrote half of everyone's favorite road-trip playlist. She framed the whole stretch as still feeling a little unreal, which tracks with the year she's having.

## What's actually on the tour bus

Our favorite bit was the least glamorous one. Her bus essentials, per the interview: a book, her Bible, coloring books, and her dog. No elaborate ritual, no wellness arsenal — a dog and something to color. For a tour this size, there's something reassuring about that being the honest answer.

She also talked about why she signed on for the Chase event in the first place, saying it came down to partners who let her be her authentic self. That's a theme she keeps returning to, and it lines up with how she's run everything from her merch to her setlists.

## The timing is doing her a favor

This lands in a genuinely loud week. Ella just took her fourth Country Airplay No. 1 — ["Be Her" hit the top in record time](/news/2026-05-31-be-her-country-airplay-number-one) — and tomorrow night she plays [CMA Fest opening night at Nissan Stadium](/news/2026-06-03-ella-langley-cma-fest-2026). An intimate Opry showcase tucked between a radio milestone and a stadium debut is about the best snapshot of where she is right now: still small-room reachable, already arena-sized.
