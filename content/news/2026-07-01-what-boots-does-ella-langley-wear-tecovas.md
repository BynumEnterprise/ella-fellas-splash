# The Boots Ella Langley Actually Wears — and Why Tecovas Is All Over Her Tour

If you've watched any Dandelion Tour clip and wondered what boots Ella Langley wears, the short answer is Tecovas — and it's not a one-off styling choice. The Austin-based western boot brand is now an official sponsor of the tour, and the two have been circling each other long enough that this reads less like a paycheck and more like a fan finally getting the keys to the brand she already loved.

## How the Tecovas Partnership Came Together

Per [WWD](https://wwd.com/footwear-news/shoe-trends/ella-langley-tecovas-relationship-cowboy-boots-1238983879/), the relationship built up organically over a couple of years. Ella kept turning up in Tecovas at shows and in videos, and she was one of the first artists to wear the brand's Loretta boot before it hit stores. When her team came to Tecovas around "Choosin' Texas," signing on as an official Dandelion Tour sponsor was the obvious next move for a partnership that was already real.

It goes past footwear, too. Ella asked Tecovas to make hide rugs for the tour's stage and green-room setup, and the brand connected her with Kyle Bunting — the artist behind the custom hide walls and rugs in Tecovas' retail stores. So the boots you see on stage are part of a much bigger look she helped design.

## The Boots She Actually Reaches For

Tecovas even built her a custom pair for the "[Choosin' Texas](/songs/choosin-texas)" video, which is the level of attention most fans will never get. But the everyday models she wears are ones you can actually buy: the Loretta (the pointed-toe stunner she debuted early) and the Jolene show up again and again across her stage looks and the brand's own posts.

Here's the honest part, though: the exact pair matters less than the shape. Ella leans on a classic pointed or snip toe, a mid-height walking heel, and rich browns and blacks that go with denim without trying too hard. That's a look you can put together at a range of prices.
