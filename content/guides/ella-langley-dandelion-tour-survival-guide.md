The Dandelion Tour is Ella Langley's first headlining arena run. 16 dates, May through August, mostly sold out at face value. If you're going, this is the guide for getting there, getting in, getting through the show, and getting home without missing anything.

## Before the show — three weeks out

**Lock the ticket.** Resale prices are climbing every week. The pattern across 2026 country headliners has been: resale plateaus a week out, then spikes 20-30% in the final 72 hours. If you're waiting for prices to drop, you're playing the wrong game. Buy when you decide you're going.

The cheapest reliable secondary marketplace right now is TickPick — they don't charge service fees on top of the listed price, which usually saves you 15-20% versus StubHub or Vivid Seats on the same seat.

**Check the venue's bag policy.** Most arenas on the Dandelion Tour are now clear-bag only or no-bag. The exception is the amphitheaters (Cary, Wilmington, Gilford), which still allow standard small bags. Check the venue page for your specific show.

**Book the hotel within 48 hours of buying the ticket.** Hotels near these venues fill up fast for show nights. Anywhere within 1.5 miles of the venue is the move — Uber surge pricing kicks in by 9 PM on show nights and a 2-mile ride can run $35-50.

## Day-of — the timing

The Dandelion Tour show timing is consistent across the run:

- **Doors:** 6:30 PM (most arenas)
- **Opener 1** (Kameron / Dylan Marlowe, varies by date): 7:30 PM
- **Opener 2** (Gabriella Rose / Kaitlin Butts / Laci Kaye Booth): 8:15 PM
- **Ella Langley:** 9:15 PM
- **Lights up:** ~10:50 PM

Plan your dinner before 6 PM if you want to walk into the venue when doors open. The Marlowe brothers (whichever one's opening) are worth showing up for — both write their own stuff and play tight 45-minute sets.

## The setlist preview

Based on the opening nights of the tour, expect approximately 15-16 songs in a 95-minute set. The structure has been:

1. **Opens** with "loving life again" — high-energy, immediate vibe
2. **Mid-show stretch** of Hungover deep cuts (nicotine, paint the town blue, weren't for the wind)
3. **Ballad slot** (be her, or a Lori McKenna cover)
4. **Duet section** — Riley Green walks out when geography permits (Texas/Alabama dates almost always), or a stand-in for "don't mind if i do" + "you look like you love me"
5. **Encore lands on "Choosin' Texas"**

The Wednesday-night setlists tend to be the tightest. Friday/Saturday shows often add a song.

## Where to sit, what to skip

If you're buying tickets fresh, here's the price/value breakdown for the Dandelion Tour arenas:

- **Floor (rows 1-15):** Worth the premium if you can swing it. The sound mix is best here and Ella plays *to* the floor.
- **Floor (rows 16+):** Mid-tier price, but you're far enough back that the sound suffers and the view requires the screens.
- **Lower bowl (sides, sections 100-115):** The sweet spot. Better sound than back-floor, half the price of front-floor, full venue view.
- **Upper bowl:** Cheap and surprisingly good for the visuals — the stage lighting is designed to read from up here.

Avoid sections directly behind the soundboard if your venue has them. The view of the stage is blocked or partially blocked by the lighting rig in most arena configurations.

## After the show

Most arena tours empty out within 25 minutes of lights-up. The bottleneck is parking, not the venue exit. Two strategies:

- **Stay in your seat for 15 minutes.** Watch the lights come up, finish your drink, let the lot clear. You'll get out faster than the people who rushed.
- **Pre-game the exit.** Walk out during the encore (you'll catch the bigger song from the parking lot tunnel anyway), get to your car, beat the traffic.

If you want the post-show country bar scene, every city on the tour has one worth checking out. We're building [city-by-city guides](/tour) for each stop with the actual bars locals go to — not the tourist trap honky-tonks.

## What to leave at home

- Large cameras (no DSLR / GoPro at any tour venue)
- Anything in a non-clear bag at most arenas
- Outside drinks
- Hopes of a meet-and-greet — there is no official one for the Dandelion Tour
