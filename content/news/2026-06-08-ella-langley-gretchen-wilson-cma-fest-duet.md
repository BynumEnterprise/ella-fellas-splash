We told you opening night was the date we'd circled, and the part we keep replaying isn't even from Ella's own set. The biggest Ella Langley moment at CMA Fest 2026 happened after she'd already left the stage once: Gretchen Wilson walked out for her own slot on night one and brought Ella right back out for a surprise duet of "Here for the Party." If you blinked during the changeover at Nissan Stadium, you missed the best ninety seconds of the night.

## What happened on night one

CMA Fest opened Thursday, June 4 at Nissan Stadium in Nashville in front of a crowd the Tennessean put at 70,000, and it carried some extra weight: [this was the last opening night the festival will ever hold at Nissan](https://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/music/cma-music-festival/2026/06/05/cma-fest-2026-lineup-top-moments-ella-langley-jelly-roll-florida-georgia-line/90374571007/), the 25th and final year in that building before the move next door. Florida Georgia Line reunited, Jelly Roll turned his set into a sermon, and somewhere in the middle of all that, two women from two very different points in country music tore the roof off with one song.

It was supposed to be Gretchen Wilson's slot, full stop. Instead, the "Redneck Woman" singer surprised the stadium by pulling Ella back out, and the two opened with "Here for the Party," Wilson's 2004 anthem. Ella had traded the white flowy dress from her own set for an all-leather look — a pretty clear signal of what kind of song was coming. [American Songwriter described the whole place on its feet](https://americansongwriter.com/ella-langley-teams-up-with-gretchen-wilson-for-surprise-duet-during-night-one-of-cma-fest/), the two singers feeding off the crowd the whole way through. After Ella ducked off, Wilson finished her own run with "All Jacked Up" and closed on "Redneck Woman."

## Ella's own set came first

The duet hit harder because Ella had already earned the room earlier in the evening. She opened with the title track from "Dandelion," slid into fan favorite "Be Her" — the crowd beat her to the first line — and set up "You Look Like You Love Me" by teasing a story before landing on its opening line, "I was all but 22." She worked in "Loving Life Again" with a few words about how fast life can change, ran through her smash "Choosin' Texas," and closed her set with "Weren't for the Wind." Riley Green didn't appear for their duet, so the 70,000 covered his half themselves. We [previewed exactly this slot a few days out](/news/2026-06-03-ella-langley-cma-fest-2026), and she cleared the bar and then some.

## They'd done it before — at the Ryman

Here's the detail we love: this wasn't a one-off improvisation. The Tennessean noted that Ella and Wilson had performed "Here for the Party" together once before, at the Ryman Auditorium back in November 2025. So this was less a random guest spot and more two artists who already knew the song lands when they sing it side by side. There's a nice piece of country lineage in it, too — Wilson's 2004 run was the last time a new woman kicked the door in this hard, and Ella crossing 30 million monthly Spotify listeners the same week makes the pairing feel like a passing of something, not just a fun cameo.
