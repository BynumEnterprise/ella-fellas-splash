Saturday night, 71,000 seats, and Ella walked out into a Baltimore crowd that showed up early for her. That's the part that still gets us about this stadium leg -- she's the direct support, not the headliner, so she's got maybe 45 minutes and no runway to warm up. She has to land in the first eight bars every single time. From everything we know about how she's been running the Still The Problem dates, M&T Bank Stadium got exactly that: a tight, all-hits set stacked ahead of Morgan Wallen, with Gavin Adcock and Jason Scott and the High Heat also on the bill.

Here's the honest part up front. As of Sunday morning, nobody has logged the exact Baltimore order on setlist.fm yet -- the page still lists the show as upcoming with no times posted. So this isn't a play-by-play the way our headline-tour recaps are. It's what we know she's been playing on the Wallen stadium run, and we'll update the second a full Baltimore list gets posted or a fan on the floor sends us one.

## The set she's been running on the stadium leg

Across the Still The Problem stadium dates, Ella's direct-support set has held steady around 10 to 12 songs, pulled almost entirely from the Dandelion era and built to hit fast. The spine of it, per the tour's documented support sets and setlist.fm 2026 averages:

- "weren't for the wind" (her most frequent opener on this run)
- "Country Boy's Dream Girl"
- "nicotine"
- "paint the town blue"
- "Dandelion"
- "Bottom of Your Boots"
- "Be Her"
- "Loving Life Again"
- "you look like you love me" (the Riley Green duet -- crowd handles Riley's lines)
- "Choosin' Texas"

Treat that as the shape, not gospel. The middle three or four are where she swaps things night to night, and a stadium changeover clock this tight sometimes trims a song. If you were on the floor Saturday and the order ran different, that's exactly what we want to hear.

The one to watch for on the Wallen dates: she's been coming back out during Morgan's set for their 2026 duet "I Can't Love You Anymore." We haven't seen it confirmed for Baltimore specifically, so we're filing that under "hoped for, not verified" until a clip surfaces.
