Search "ella langley fan club" and you get a confusing mix of presale-code resellers and ticket sites. Here is the straight answer, because we went through it for every Dandelion Tour on-sale this year.

## There's no paid club -- but the Fan Club Presale is real

Ticketmaster runs an official **Ella Langley Fan Club Presale** before most of her headline shows (the fall-extension on-sales ran it at noon CT). The "fan club" behind it is not a paid membership -- it's her free official channels:

- **The mailing list** at [ellalangley.com](https://ellalangley.com) -- sign up once, presale codes land in your inbox before each on-sale
- **Her text community** -- Ella runs a Community number; texts go out with codes and drops
- **Her socials** -- announcements post there first, but codes come by email/text

That's it. If a site wants money for "fan club membership," close the tab. (Third-party code aggregators exist, but the codes they resell are the same ones the mailing list sends free.)

## How the presale actually plays out

For the Dandelion Tour dates this year the pattern has been: announcement, then Fan Club Presale (typically starting 10 AM-12 PM local a few days ahead), then venue/radio presales, then the public on-sale Friday. Sold-out shows -- like [Cary](/guides/ella-langley-cary-2026-set-times) and [North Charleston](/guides/ella-langley-north-charleston-2026-set-times) this month -- often never make it to public sale in good sections, which is exactly why the free presale signup matters.
