# Choosin' Texas Climbs Back to No. 1 and Rewrites the Record Book

A month after we figured its Hot 100 reign was finished, ["Choosin' Texas"](/songs/choosin-texas) is back on top. Ella Langley's crossover anthem returned to No. 1 on this week's Billboard Hot 100, pushing its total to **11 weeks at the summit** — and with that eleventh week, Ella now owns the longest-leading Hot 100 No. 1 by a woman with a country hit. It is the song's first week back at the top in more than a month; it last led the chart dated May 18.

## The 1977 Record That Finally Fell

The mark she just passed is almost old enough to be her grandparents' record. Debby Boone's "You Light Up My Life" spent 10 weeks at No. 1 on the Hot 100 back in 1977 — a song that also reached No. 4 on Hot Country Songs — and for 49 years no woman with a country hit had stayed up there longer. "Choosin' Texas" has now done it, becoming the sole record holder rather than sharing the line. For a song that started as a slow-building radio cut, sitting alone above a number that has held since the Carter administration is the kind of thing that's hard to overstate.

## Six Trips to No. 1 — A Record of Its Own

What makes this run so unusual isn't just the total, it's the shape of it. Per [Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/lists/ella-langley-choosin-texas-hot-100-number-one-11th-week/), "Choosin' Texas" has now logged **six separate stays at No. 1**: the weeks dated February 14; March 7 and March 21–28; April 11–25; May 9–23; and now this week. That's the most distinct returns to the top during a single release cycle in Hot 100 history, ahead of the five separate reigns by Morgan Wallen's "Last Night" in 2023 and Harry Styles' "As It Was" in 2022. Only Mariah Carey's "All I Want for Christmas Is You," with its eight seasonal comebacks since 2019, has bounced back to No. 1 more times — and that one has the calendar working for it every December.

## Why It Keeps Coming Back

Songs don't claw their way back to No. 1 six times by accident. "Choosin' Texas" has stayed in the middle of the conversation all spring and into summer — the Dandelion Tour selling out arena after arena, the constant radio play, and the steady streaming numbers that don't fall off the way a one-week novelty would. This is also far from her only record this month. ["Choosin' Texas" already set the Hot Country Songs mark for a solo woman](/news/2026-06-28-ella-langley-choosin-texas-hot-country-songs-record), and ["Be Her" became her longest-running Country Airplay No. 1](/news/2026-06-27-be-her-longest-country-airplay-no-1) a few days before that. Three records, three different charts, all inside a couple of weeks.

It also caps a year that already included an ACM sweep on May 17, where she took Female Artist of the Year, Single of the Year, Song of the Year, and Artist-Songwriter of the Year.
