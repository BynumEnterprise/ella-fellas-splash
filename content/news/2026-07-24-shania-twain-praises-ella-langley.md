## Why Shania Twain Says Ella Langley "Commands Respect"

When it comes to a **Shania Twain Ella Langley** co-sign, this is about as big as it gets. The best-selling female country artist of all time just spent part of a new interview talking about why Ella is taking over the genre — and she did not hedge. Twain called Langley authentic, called her sound original, and landed on a line that country fans have been passing around all week: Ella "really does seem very unapologetic, and I think that commands respect."

## The Quick Version

In a new Rolling Stone profile tied to her own upcoming album, Shania Twain was asked about the artists breaking through right now, and Ella Langley came up fast. Twain admitted she has never actually met Ella — this is a legend watching a newcomer from across the room, same as the rest of us — but she had clearly been paying attention. As Whiskey Riff and American Songwriter both highlighted, Twain zeroed in on the one thing that is hardest to fake: that Ella sounds like herself and nobody else.

## What Shania Actually Said

The heart of it is authenticity. "She seems very authentic," Twain said, describing Langley as someone who does not come across as if she is trying to be another artist or trying to write a song with an agenda. Then came the quote everyone pulled: "She really does seem very unapologetic, and I think that commands respect. It really does."

Twain also framed it as a return to something she felt the format had lost. Ella, she said, brings her back to "the early country that I grew up with and was singing as a child," adding that "Ella Langley to me is really country Western, and I've missed that in the genre." Coming from the woman who rewrote what a female country star could be in the '90s, "I've missed that" is a heavy compliment. American Songwriter summed up her read on Ella in three words: "I believe her."

## Why This Praise Lands Right Now

Timing is everything, and Twain's praise arrives while Ella is in the middle of a genuinely historic run. "Choosin' Texas" has been sitting at No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100 for 14 weeks — long enough to tie for the longest-running chart-topper by a female artist this century — and Langley became the first woman to lead the Hot 100, Country Airplay, and Hot Country Songs charts at the same time. If you want the story behind that song, we broke down [what "Choosin' Texas" is really about](/news/2026-07-22-choosin-texas-meaning).

So this is not one star being polite about a hopeful. It is the most successful woman in country history looking at the most successful woman in country right now and saying the success is earned. That is the part that "commands respect" really points at — not the numbers, but the fact that Ella got there writing exactly the songs she wanted to write.

## The Throughline: She Writes Women Who Know What They Want

What Twain is describing shows up in the songs themselves. It is the woman making the first move in ["You Look Like You Love Me"](/songs/you-look-like-you-love-me), the clear-eyed reckoning of ["Weren't for the Wind"](/news/2026-07-21-werent-for-the-wind-meaning), and the small-town loyalty of "Choosin' Texas." None of them sound focus-grouped, and that is the whole point. Ella has said she just writes what feels true to her — the same instinct Twain built a career on.

There is a nice full-circle piece to this, too. Ella has openly counted Shania as an influence and even [covered a Twain classic on the road](/news/2026-06-27-ella-langley-shania-twain-cover) earlier this summer. Getting the compliment back, from the source, is the kind of moment that tends to stick with an artist.
