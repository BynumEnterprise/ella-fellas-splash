There are two completely different answers to this question in 2026, and mixing them up is how people miss her.

At her **own** Dandelion Tour shows she's the headliner and goes on around **9:00 PM**. At a **Morgan Wallen stadium date** she's direct support, the bill starts in the afternoon, and she's on right before Wallen, after dark. Same summer, same artist, four-hour difference. Here's every city.

## The short answer

**At an Ella Langley headline show:**

- **Doors:** about an hour before the listed start (roughly 6:00 PM)
- **Show starts:** 7:00 PM on every remaining 2026 date
- **Two openers** play first
- **Ella takes the stage:** around 9:00 PM

**At a Morgan Wallen stadium show:** she's the direct-support act — on immediately before Wallen, after sunset. The listed gates time (4:30 PM in Baltimore, for example) is when the *first* opener plays, not when Ella does.

One caveat we're going to keep repeating: **exact slot times are not announced in advance.** Venues post the night-of running order on the day. The times above are the pattern this tour has actually run, per [Holler's Dandelion Tour guide](https://holler.country/news/general/ella-langley-2026-the-dandelion-tour-everything-you-need-to-know/) and the venue listings — not a promise. If her set is why you bought the ticket, build in a buffer.

## Don't skip the openers

Ella has been deliberate about who rides with her. [Rolling Stone reported](https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/ella-langley-dandelion-tour-opening-acts-1235580940/) that she stacked the Dandelion Tour's support slots with female artists — some without major-label deals — which is still an unusual booking call for an arena tour. [Kaitlin Butts](/guides/who-is-kaitlin-butts) and [Laci Kaye Booth](/guides/who-is-laci-kaye-booth) are not "show up late" acts. Getting in at 7 is the point.
