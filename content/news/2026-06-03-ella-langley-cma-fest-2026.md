Of all the dates on Ella's loaded summer calendar, this is the one we circled first: she's playing CMA Fest 2026 on opening night. Thursday, June 4 at Nissan Stadium in Nashville, Ella Langley takes the biggest stage in country music's biggest week, and given the year she's had, this feels less like a festival slot and more like a homecoming the whole format has been waiting for.

## When Ella plays at CMA Fest 2026

CMA Fest runs June 4 through 7, with the marquee nightly concerts happening inside [Nissan Stadium](https://cmafest.com/stages/nissan-stadium/). Ella is on the Thursday, June 4 bill — the night that kicks the whole stadium run off. She shares opening night with Jason Aldean, Tucker Wetmore, Deana Carter and Gretchen Wilson, which is a pretty wild spread of country across the decades on one stage.

A couple of things worth knowing if you're heading down or watching from home. The stadium shows are a rotating revue, so each artist gets a tight set rather than a full headline slot — think a handful of songs, not a two-hour show. Set times for individual artists usually firm up close to showtime, and the whole thing gets filmed for the network special that airs later in the summer, so even if you can't be there Thursday, you'll likely catch her set eventually.

## What we expect from her set

If there's a short window to make an impression, Ella's holding the strongest hand in the building. "Choosin' Texas" has been parked at the top of the charts for most of the year, "Be Her" just took the radio crown, and her duet with Morgan Wallen, "I Can't Love You Anymore," has become a genuine stadium singalong. We'd bet the set leans on those, and a CMA Fest crowd is exactly the kind of room where the call-and-response moments in "Choosin' Texas" land hardest.

It's also a full-circle moment worth sitting with. Two years ago Ella was playing the smaller daytime stages around Nashville during CMA Fest week. Walking out under the lights at Nissan Stadium on opening night, the same summer she swept the ACMs, is about as steep a climb as this format produces.
