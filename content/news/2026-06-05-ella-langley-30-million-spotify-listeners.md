# Ella's Now Second Only to Wallen Among Country Artists on Spotify

We've been keeping a running tally of Ella's chart year, and here's the newest line for the ledger: she has crossed 30 million monthly Spotify listeners, which now makes her the second-most-streamed country artist on the platform behind only Morgan Wallen. [Parade reported the milestone on June 4](https://parade.com/entertainment/country-star-ella-langley-30-million-monthly-spotify-listeners-dandelion-tour-2026), and it lands at a moment when she's also got the biggest country song of the year and a tour rolling through arenas all summer.

If you've followed her story, the number is hard to wrap your head around. Three years ago she was sitting under a million monthly listeners. Now she's ahead of names like Zach Bryan, Luke Combs and Chris Stapleton on the same chart, with only Wallen still in front of her.

## What's Actually Driving the Number

The engine here is "Choosin' Texas," and it isn't slowing down. The single has now spent 26 weeks at No. 1 on Billboard's Hot Country Songs chart, and it recently passed Wallen's "Last Night" to become the fifth-longest-running leader in that chart's history. That's the kind of staying power that quietly compounds streaming totals week after week.

It's not a one-song story either. On the latest Digital Song Sales chart, "Choosin' Texas" held the top spot with more than 8,600 copies sold, while "Be Her" climbed to No. 2 with about 3,500, per Luminate data Parade cited. All told, Ella occupied four spots inside the Digital Song Sales top 10 that week. When that many of your songs are moving at once, 30 million monthly listeners starts to look less like a spike and more like a floor.

And don't forget the album underneath all of it. *Dandelion* debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 back in April, carrying "Be Her," the Miranda Lambert duet "Butterfly Season," and "Choosin' Texas" itself. The catalog is deep enough now that casual listeners keep finding new doors in.

## Where This Sits in Her 2026

Put it next to everything else and the picture gets a little absurd, in the best way. She swept big categories at the ACM and American Music Awards earlier this year, she became the first woman to hold the top two spots on the Hot 100 at the same time, and she's spent the spring doing all of it while touring. The streaming milestone isn't a separate achievement so much as the scoreboard catching up to a year that's already been historic. We've been tracking [every Billboard record she's set this year](/about), and this one fits right in.

For us as fans, the most fun part is what 30 million listeners actually means on the ground: bigger sing-alongs, faster sellouts, and a lot of people walking into the rest of the [Dandelion Tour](/tour) already knowing every word. Second-most-streamed country artist in the world, behind one person. That's where Ella Langley is in June 2026.
