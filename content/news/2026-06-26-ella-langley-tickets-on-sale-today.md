# Ella Langley Tickets Are On Sale Now: Your Dandelion Tour Game Plan

If you got shut out of yesterday's presale, take a breath -- this is the morning that matters. **Ella Langley tickets** for the new fall leg of the Dandelion Tour go to general on-sale today, Friday, June 26 at 10 a.m. local time, and after the way Thursday's presale went, a lot of us are walking into this one a little gun-shy. Below is exactly what happened, what to do differently this morning, and how to actually land seats without overpaying.

## What Happened in the Presale

Thursday's artist presale did not go smoothly. As fans flooded in, Ticketmaster buckled -- buyers reported "Bad Gateway" errors, getting booted from the queue, and watching their place in line jump from a few thousand to well over 124,000 after the system kicked them and made them rejoin. The crush was bad enough that the presale was paused while the system recovered, and the problems spilled across other high-demand on-sales happening the same afternoon.

Ella addressed it directly on her Instagram story, telling fans the team was aware of the technical issues and thanking everyone for their patience while it got sorted. None of that is much comfort if you spent an hour staring at an error screen -- but it does mean plenty of good inventory rolled over to this morning's general sale.

## How to Play the 10 a.m. On-Sale

A few things that genuinely help on a high-demand on-sale:

- **Log in early.** Be signed into your account and in the queue a few minutes before 10 a.m. local, not at 10:01.
- **One device, one tab.** Multiple tabs often flag you as a bot and bounce you. Pick your fastest connection and stay put.
- **Decide your price ceiling first.** Inventory moves in seconds; you do not want to be doing math while the timer runs.
- **Don't panic-refresh.** If you get a queue number, hold it. Refreshing usually sends you to the back.
