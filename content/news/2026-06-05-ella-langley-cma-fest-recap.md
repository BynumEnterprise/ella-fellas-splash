We told you opening night was the date we'd circled, and Ella didn't waste it. Thursday at Nissan Stadium she walked out for her own set on the first night of [CMA Fest 2026](/news/2026-06-03-ella-langley-cma-fest-2026), admitted she was a little nervous in front of that stadium crowd, and then went and earned the room twice over — once on her own, and once in a surprise nobody had on their card.

## Her set

For her slot, Ella leaned right into the song that started all of this, breaking into ["You Look Like You Love Me"](/songs/you-look-like-you-love-me) early and letting the stadium carry the hook back at her. She closed out with ["Weren't for the Wind"](/songs/werent-for-the-wind), the 2024 cut that's quietly become one of the steadiest singalongs in her catalog. It was a tight stadium-revue set rather than a full headline night — exactly the kind of short window we figured she'd attack — and on her second-ever time playing Nissan, she handled it like someone who's done this for years.

## The Gretchen Wilson moment

Then came the part we didn't see coming. Later in the night, Gretchen Wilson pulled Ella back out and the two of them tore into "Here for the Party." It wasn't a total first — they'd done the song together at the Ryman back in November — but on a stadium stage on opening night, it landed as the most barn-burning few minutes of the evening, the kind of crowd-on-its-feet stretch that ends up in the network special this summer. Two generations of Alabama-and-attitude country trading a chorus that was built for exactly this: it's the moment people are going to talk about from night one.

## What's next

Ella barely gets a breath. She's straight into the Morgan Wallen stadium run, with Pittsburgh's Acrisure Stadium up on Saturday, June 6, then [Railbird Festival in Lexington on Sunday, June 7](/news/2026-06-04-ella-langley-railbird-festival-2026), where she sits third from the top behind Tyler Childers.
