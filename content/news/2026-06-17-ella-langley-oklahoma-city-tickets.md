## Best spots at the Zoo Amphitheatre

The Zoo Amphitheatre is mostly a general-admission lawn venue, so "best seats" really means best position. A few things we've learned about outdoor country shows here:

- **Down front, center** is where you want to be if you're there to be *in* it — Ella works the lip of the stage and the energy up close is the point. Get there for doors at 6:30 to claim your patch.
- **Mid-lawn, slightly elevated** is our value pick. You can see the whole stage, the sound carries cleanly, and you've got room to actually dance instead of being packed shoulder to shoulder.
- **Back of the lawn** is the relaxed play — bring a low chair if the venue allows it, and you'll still hear everything fine in a room this size.

## Getting there: parking and timing

The Zoo Amphitheatre sits inside the Adventure District in northeast OKC, next to the Oklahoma City Zoo, so on-site parking is plentiful but the exit crawl after a sold show is real — plan for it. The venue is an easy shot off I-44 and Martin Luther King Ave. Arrive close to doors if you care about a good lawn spot, since GA means first in, first picked.
