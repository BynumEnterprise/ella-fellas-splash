Last week [we said we knew where we'd be on Monday](/news/2026-07-16-ella-langley-choosin-texas-13-weeks-hot-100). Well, Monday came, and the thing we were waiting on actually happened: "Choosin' Texas" logged its fourteenth week at No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100, on the chart dated July 25. Per [Billboard](https://www.billboard.com/lists/ella-langley-choosin-texas-hot-100-number-one-14th-week/), that ties Whitney Houston's "I Will Always Love You" and Mariah Carey's "We Belong Together" for the most weeks at No. 1 among non-holiday songs by women with no male-billed acts.

Read that list again. "I Will Always Love You." "We Belong Together." "Choosin' Texas." A country song written by Ella Langley, Miranda Lambert, Luke Dick and JoyBeth Taylor now sits level with two of the most famous chart runs in pop history, per [MusicRow](https://musicrow.com/2026/07/ella-langleys-choosin-texas-makes-history-with-14th-week-atop-billboard-hot-100/). [Variety](https://variety.com/2026/music/news/ella-langley-choosin-texas-ties-whitney-houston-mariah-carey-records-1236816760/) covered it too — this one traveled well past the country outlets today.

## The numbers behind week 14

For the July 10-16 tracking week, Billboard reports 24 million official U.S. streams (down 4%), 51 million in radio airplay audience (up 1%) and 7,000 downloads sold, essentially even. That radio number ticking up in week 14 is the part we keep staring at — songs this deep into a run usually fade everywhere at once, and this one is still growing on the airwaves while ["Loving Life Again" spins up as the next single](/news/2026-07-14-ella-langley-loving-life-again-radio-single) behind it.

## What next Monday means

Here's the stakes now: one more week on top and "Choosin' Texas" stands alone — the longest-leading non-holiday Hot 100 No. 1 ever by a woman with no male-billed acts. Among all songs by women, only Mariah's "All I Want for Christmas Is You" (22 weeks) has led longer. So yes, we'll be refreshing the chart again next Monday. At this point it's a weekly ritual around here.
