## Those earrings you keep spotting on the Dandelion Tour are finally shoppable

If you've been staring at Ella's stage looks all summer trying to figure out where the jewelry comes from, you can stop squinting. Ella has teamed up with Hillbetty on an exclusive collection built around two pieces we've been seeing on her for a while now -- the Greta Choker and the Stella Hoops. She teased it on her Instagram Story, and Hillbetty confirmed the launch on their own account with the details.

## What's in the collection

The collab centers on two signature pieces. The Greta Choker is the statement piece -- a bold layered choker that's become a fixture of Ella's current era. The Stella Hoops are the classic gold hoops she pairs it with. According to Hillbetty, the two originally came out of a Hillbetty and Stella65 collaboration, where the Greta necklace and Stella hoops together became one of the brand's most recognizable combinations. Now they're packaged as an Ella-branded set.

## Why fans already know these pieces

Here's the fun part for those of us who watch every video frame by frame: you've already seen this jewelry. The Greta Choker and Stella Hoops show up in the ["Be Her" music video](/news/2026-06-16-be-her-ella-langley-meaning), and they've been on Ella regularly across [the Dandelion Tour](/tour). So this isn't a random merch drop -- it's the actual jewelry from the era being made available to buy. That's a different thing entirely, and it's the kind of detail that makes it worth grabbing before a limited run sells through.

## It fits a bigger pattern this year

The jewelry lands in the middle of Ella's biggest year yet. She swept the 2026 ACM Awards with seven trophies including Female Artist of the Year, "Choosin' Texas" just logged an eleventh week at No. 1 on the Hot 100, and her lifestyle partnerships keep stacking up. Just this week we covered [the Tecovas boots she wears](/news/2026-07-01-what-boots-does-ella-langley-wear-tecovas) after the brand signed on as an official Dandelion Tour sponsor. The Hillbetty collab is the same story from a different angle -- the stuff Ella actually wears is becoming stuff you can own.

Hillbetty's Instagram is the place to check stock and pricing, since it's a limited collection and we'd expect the popular pieces to move fast.
