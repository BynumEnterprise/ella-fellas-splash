## Best seats at Dickies Arena

Dickies is one of the better-sounding arenas on this tour, and it's smaller than the football-stadium stops, so there's no genuinely bad seat. That said:

- **Floor** is the move if you want to be in it — Ella works the front of the stage and the energy down there on a finale night is the whole point. Expect to pay the most and to stand the entire show.
- **Lower bowl, sides (sections near the stage)** is our value pick. You're elevated enough to see the full stage production but still close, and you'll actually have a seat when the ballads hit.
- **Upper deck** is the budget play and honestly fine here — the room is intimate enough that the back rows still feel connected. This is where the sub-$100 resale lives.

For a country show with this much singing-along, we'd take a great lower-bowl seat over a so-so floor spot every time.

## Getting there: parking and timing

Dickies Arena sits in the Will Rogers Memorial Center complex on the west side of Fort Worth, near the Cultural District. On-site and adjacent lots fill fast for a sold-out Saturday, so either pre-book a parking pass or plan to use a rideshare drop-off and budget extra time for the post-show crawl out. Doors at 6:30 give you room to grab merch and settle in before Laci Kaye Booth opens — get there for doors if you care about the openers, because Kaitlin Butts is worth catching in her own right.
