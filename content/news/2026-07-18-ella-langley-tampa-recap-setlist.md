Friday night in Tampa, and Ella Langley walked out in front of one of the biggest rooms of the summer -- a sold-out Raymond James Stadium, direct support for Morgan Wallen on the Still The Problem Tour. Stadium support is a different animal than her own Dandelion headline nights: 45 minutes or so, no time to wander, every song has to land in the first eight bars. From everything we know about how she's been running this leg, she came out to do exactly that.

Here's the honest part up front: as of Saturday morning, nobody has logged the exact Tampa order on setlist.fm yet. So this is not a play-by-play the way our headline recaps are -- it's what we know she's been playing on the Wallen stadium run, and we'll update the moment a full Tampa list gets posted or a fan sends one in.

## The set she's been running on the stadium leg

Across the Still The Problem stadium dates, Ella's direct-support set has held pretty steady around 10 to 12 songs, pulled straight from the Dandelion era. The spine of it, per the tour's documented support sets:

1. "Hell at Night" (the BigXthaPlug crossover, used as the cold open)
2. "Country Boy's Dream Girl"
3. "nicotine"
4. "paint the town blue"
5. "Dandelion"
6. "Bottom of Your Boots"
7. "Be Her"
8. "Loving Life Again"
9. "you look like you love me" (the Riley Green duet)
10. "Choosin' Texas"
11. "weren't for the wind"

Treat that as the shape, not the gospel -- the middle of the set is where she swaps things night to night, and a stadium crowd this size sometimes gets a shortened version if the changeover clock is tight. If you were on the floor Friday and the order ran different, that's exactly the stuff we want.

## What we're watching for from Tampa

A few things that would make the Tampa night stand out if they happened, and that fan accounts usually surface first. Whether she opened cold on "Hell at Night" or flipped it -- that opener has been the tell all run. Whether "you look like you love me" got any surprise help; it's a duet built for a guest, and a Wallen stadium bill is the kind of place a surprise walk-out happens. And how "weren't for the wind" closed it out -- in a 65,000-seat football stadium, that singalong is a genuinely different sound than it is in an amphitheater.
